What is Partial Knee Replacement?

PKR, or unicompartmental knee replacement, is a minimally invasive surgery to treat knee osteoarthritis that affects only one of the three knee compartments (medial, lateral, or patellofemoral). In this procedure, the surgeon removes only the damaged tissue and replaces it with a prosthetic implant, allowing for more natural movement than a total knee replacement.

This procedure has grown in popularity due to its less invasive nature and quicker recovery times, especially compared to total knee replacement or resurfacing surgery.

Benefits

One of the major advantages of this procedure is that it offers a more targeted approach to knee surgery. The main advantages of partial knee replacement include:

  1. Less Invasive Surgery: Since only part of the knee is replaced, the incision is smaller, resulting in less disruption to healthy tissues, tendons, and muscles.
  2. Quicker Recovery Time: Patients can expect a faster recovery than total knee replacement. This is because the procedure preserves the undamaged parts of the knee, reducing the healing time.
  3. More Natural Knee Movement: The preserved knee parts allow for more natural movement post-surgery, enhancing overall mobility.
  4. Reduced Pain: Knee pain relief with partial replacement is significant, as the procedure directly targets the areas causing discomfort, providing effective long-term relief.
  5. Shorter Hospital Stay: In most cases, patients can return home sooner, often within 24 to 48 hours after surgery.

These benefits of partial knee replacement make it a preferred option for those who qualify, particularly patients who have damage confined to one knee compartment.

The Procedure for PKR

The surgical process typically follows these steps:

  1. Preparation: The surgeon makes a small incision over the knee to access the damaged compartment.
  2. Resurfacing the Knee: The surgeon removes the damaged bone and cartilage from the affected part of the knee.
  3. Implantation: A prosthetic component is carefully placed in the knee to replace the damaged areas, restoring smooth joint function.
  4. Wound Closure and Recovery: The surgical incision is closed, and the patient is closely monitored throughout recovery.

Because only part of the knee is replaced, this surgery typically takes less time than a total knee replacement. It also leads to quicker post-operative recovery, as less tissue is disturbed.

Recovery Process

Recovery after PKR is generally faster and less painful than total knee replacement surgery. Most patients can return to normal activities, including walking, within a few weeks, although full recovery may take up to six months.

Key Aspects of Recovery:

  1. Physical Therapy: Just like knee resurfacing surgery, physical therapy is critical in restoring strength and mobility. A structured rehabilitation program will begin shortly after surgery to encourage movement and prevent stiffness.
  2. Pain Management: Pain is typically managed with over-the-counter medications or prescribed pain relief. Ice packs and elevation can also reduce swelling in the knee.
  3. Return to Daily Activities: Most patients can walk unassisted within a few weeks of surgery. Low-impact exercises, such as swimming or cycling, can help promote healing and improve knee function.
  4. Follow-Up Care: Regular check-ups with your surgeon will ensure the knee is healing correctly and that the implant functions as expected.

Overall, recovery after partial knee replacement tends to be more comfortable, with a quicker return to normal activities when compared to total knee replacement.

Comparing Partial Knee Replacement and Knee Resurfacing Surgery

While partial knee replacement and knee resurfacing surgery are sometimes used interchangeably, there are subtle differences between the two procedures. In partial knee replacement, the surgeon replaces only the damaged part of the knee with an implant. In contrast, knee resurfacing surgery typically involves smoothing or resurfacing worn-down cartilage in the knee joint.

Both procedures aim to relieve pain and restore function, but your doctor will decide which option is best for you depending on the severity and location of your knee damage. For many patients, partial knee replacement provides a more durable and long-lasting solution.
