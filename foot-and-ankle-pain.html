Common Causes of Foot and Ankle Pain

Understanding the underlying cause of foot and ankle pain is critical to finding the right treatment.

  • Plantar Fasciitis: A leading cause of heel pain, plantar fasciitis occurs when the plantar fascia, a band of tissue running along the bottom of your foot, becomes inflamed.
  • Arthritis: Osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis can affect the foot and ankle, causing stiffness, swelling, and pain.
  • Tendonitis: The Achilles tendon, which connects your calf muscles to your heel bone, can become inflamed due to overuse, leading to pain and limited movement.
  • Fractures and Sprains: An injury caused by twisting or direct trauma can lead to fractures or sprains, both common causes of foot and ankle pain.
  • Bunions and Hammertoes: Deformities in the foot structure can lead to discomfort and pain in the toes and ball of the foot.

Regardless of the condition, early intervention by a foot and ankle surgery specialist is crucial for preventing long-term complications.

Non-Surgical Treatment Options

For many patients, non-invasive treatment options can provide relief. Our best orthopedic doctor for foot pain focuses on conservative methods as a first-line approach. These may include:

  1. Physical Therapy: Exercises and stretches that focus on strengthening muscles around the foot and ankle, improving flexibility, and relieving pain.
  2. Custom Orthotics: Specially designed shoe inserts to provide support and alleviate pressure on the feet.
  3. Medication: Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) may be prescribed to reduce inflammation and manage pain effectively.
  4. Injections: Corticosteroid injections can effectively alleviate inflammation in conditions such as arthritis and tendonitis.

Foot and Ankle Surgery for Severe Conditions

In cases where non-surgical treatments fail, or if the condition is severe, surgical intervention may be necessary. Our foot and ankle surgery specialists in Ranchi are experienced in performing various procedures to address complex foot and ankle problems.

Common Surgical Procedures:

  • Plantar Fasciotomy: This surgery is recommended for patients with chronic heel pain due to plantar fasciitis when other treatments fail. It involves cutting part of the plantar fascia to relieve tension.
  • Achilles Tendon Repair: For patients with severe tendon injuries, this surgery can restore proper function to the tendon and alleviate pain.
  • Ankle Fusion or Replacemen: For patients with advanced arthritis in the ankle, fusion surgery may be necessary to stabilize the joint. In some cases, an ankle replacement might be a better option for restoring mobility.
  • Bunion Removal: Bunions, which are bony bumps on the base of the big toe, can be surgically removed if they cause persistent pain or interfere with daily activities.

When Should You Consult a Foot and Ankle Specialist

Knowing when to seek professional care is key to preventing further damage. Consider seeing a foot and ankle surgery specialist if you encounter:

  • Persistent pain that worsens over time.
  • Trouble walking or putting weight on your foot or ankle.
  • Swelling, redness, or signs of infection.
  • Numbness or tingling in the foot.

Prompt consultation with the best orthopedic doctor for foot pain in Ranchi can help identify the root cause of your discomfort and get you started on the path to recovery.
