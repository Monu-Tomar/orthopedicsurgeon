Causes of Knee Pain

Osteoarthritis

Age-related wear and tear in the knee joint causes inflammation, stiffness, and chronic pain, particularly in older adults.

Ligament Injuries

Injuries to knee ligaments, such as the ACL or MCL, are common in sports and can cause instability and sharp pain.

Meniscus Tears

A tear in the knee‚Äôs cartilage, often from twisting or sudden movements, leads to pain, swelling, and difficulty bending the knee.

Tendinitis

Inflammation of knee tendons due to overuse or repetitive stress, commonly seen in athletes and individuals with active lifestyles.

Bursitis

Inflammation of the bursae, small fluid-filled sacs in the knee joint, caused by repeated pressure or injury.

Patellar Dislocation

When the kneecap shifts out of place, often due to trauma or structural issues, it causes pain and limited mobility.

Symptoms of Knee Pain

Swelling and Stiffness
The knee may appear swollen and feel stiff, especially after physical activity or prolonged rest.
Sharp or Dull Pain
Pain can range from a mild ache to sharp, intense pain, often worsening with movement or weight-bearing.
Limited Range of Motion
Difficulty bending or straightening the knee fully, leading to restricted movement.
Instability or Weakness
A feeling that the knee may ‚Äúgive way‚Äù or buckle, especially during walking or standing.
Popping or Clicking Sounds
Sounds or sensations in the knee during movement, which may indicate cartilage or ligament issues.
Pain When Climbing Stairs or Standing
Increased discomfort when bearing weight, climbing stairs, or standing up from a seated position.

When to See a Doctor for Knee Pain?

Persistent Pain Lasting More Than a Few Days

Knee pain that doesn‚Äôt improve with rest or home care may require a professional evaluation.

Severe Swelling or Redness

Swelling, redness, or warmth around the knee could indicate inflammation or infection and should be assessed promptly.

Inability to Bear Weight or Walk

Difficulty standing, walking, or putting weight on the knee could indicate a serious injury.

Pain Following an Injury or Fall

Knee pain after a fall, sports injury, or accident should be examined for potential fractures, ligament tears, or other damage.

Limited Movement or Stiffness

Inability to fully bend or straighten the knee may suggest an underlying issue that needs medical attention.

Knee Giving Way or Feeling Unstable

A knee that feels like it might "give out" could indicate ligament damage or structural issues.
