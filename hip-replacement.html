What is Hip Replacement Surgery?

Hip surgery, or hip arthroplasty, is a medical procedure in which a deteriorated hip joint is replaced with a specially designed artificial implant. It is primarily performed to alleviate pain and restore mobility for individuals dealing with advanced arthritis, hip fractures, or other joint deterioration.

The procedure involves removing the affected ections of the hip joint and replacing them with a prosthesis made of metal, plastic, or ceramic. The implants are crafted to imitate the natural movement of a healthy hip, helping to enhance joint functionality, reduce discomfort, and improve the patient’s overall quality of life.

Who Needs Hip Replacement Surgery?

WOsteoarthritis Patients

Rheumatoid Arthritis Patients

Individuals with chronic inflammation in the hip joint causing pain, stiffness, and deformity.

Hip Fracture Patients

Those with serious hip fractures, especially in elderly patients, may need hip replacement for recovery.

Avascular Necrosis

Patients with reduced blood flow to the hip bone, leading to joint damage.

Limited Mobility

Individuals experiencing difficulty walking, standing, or performing everyday tasks due to hip pain.

Failed Previous Hip Surgery

Patients who have had previous hip surgeries that have not been successful or require revision.

Types of Hip Replacement Surgeries

Total Hip Replacement (THR)

The entire hip joint is replaced with prosthetic components, including the ball (femoral head) and socket (acetabulum).

Partial Hip Replacement (Hemiarthroplasty)

Only the damaged femoral head (ball) is replaced, while the socket remains intact, typically performed after certain fractures.

Hip Resurfacing

The damaged surfaces of the hip joint are capped with metal prostheses, preserving more of the patient’s natural bone.

Revision Hip Replacement

A follow-up surgery to replace or repair a previous hip replacement that has worn out or failed.

FAQs

How long does a hip replacement surgery take?
The duration of the surgery is generally between 1 to 2 hours. It depends on how complex the procedure is and the patient’s health.
How much time is taken for recovery after hip replacement surgery?
Most patients can begin light activities within 3 to 6 weeks after surgery, with full recovery taking about 3 to 6 months, depending on the individual.
How long does a hip replacement last?
Modern hip replacements typically last 15 to 20 years or longer, depending on the patient’s activity level and implant type.
Will I need physical therapy after hip replacement?
Yes, physical therapy is crucial for recovery, helping restore strength, mobility, and range of motion.
Can I resume normal activities after hip replacement surgery?
Yes, after recovery, most patients can resume everyday activities like walking, climbing stairs, and even low-impact exercises..
