What Is Elbow Arthroscopy Surgery?

Elbow arthroscopy surgery involves the use of a small camera, or arthroscope, to examine and treat issues within the elbow joint. Through small incisions, the surgeon inserts the arthroscope and surgical tools to repair damaged tissues, remove inflamed or scar tissue, or remove bone spurs. Elbow arthroscopy surgery can address various conditions, from arthritis to ligament damage and sports injuries.

Unlike traditional open surgery, elbow arthroscopy offers a less invasive approach, minimizing trauma to surrounding tissues and muscles. The benefits of elbow arthroscopy surgery are vast, as it speeds up recovery time and minimizes scarring.

Common Conditions Treated in this Surgery

A minimally invasive procedure performed to treat various conditions affecting the elbow joint, including:

  1. Sports Injuries: Athletes often face elbow injuries, such as ligament tears or tendonitis, that can benefit from the minimally invasive approach of arthroscopy.
  2. Chronic Elbow Pain:Conditions like arthritis or bursitis can cause persistent pain, which can be alleviated with surgical intervention.
  3. Loose Bodies in the Elbow Joint: Fragments of bone or cartilage can become loose, leading to joint instability or discomfort. These can be removed using elbow arthroscopy.
  4. Elbow Joint Stiffness Scar tissue or inflammation may restrict movement, which elbow arthroscopy can address by removing the problematic tissue.
