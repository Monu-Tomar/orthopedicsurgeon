What is Meniscus Tear?

A meniscus tear is an injury to the cartilage in the knee that acts as a cushion between the thigh bone (femur) and shinbone (tibia). The meniscus helps absorb shock and stabilize the knee joint. A tear in this cartilage usually occurs due to sudden twisting, pivoting, or heavy lifting, often during sports or physical activities.

Meniscus tears can also result from age-related degeneration, making the cartilage more prone to tearing over time. Symptoms of a meniscus tear include knee pain, swelling, stiffness, and difficulty moving the knee fully. Proper diagnosis and treatment are crucial to restoring knee function.

Types of Hip Replacement Surgeries

✓

Radial Tear

This is the most common type of meniscus tear, occurring along the inner edge of the meniscus. It usually requires surgical repair as it affects the weight-bearing portion of the knee.

✓

Horizontal Tear

A tear that runs parallel to the tibia‚Äôs top surface, often caused by degeneration. It can sometimes be treated with non-surgical methods but may require surgery if severe.

✓

Flap Tear

A small piece of the meniscus becomes loose, creating a flap that can catch in the joint, causing pain and locking. Arthroscopic surgery is often needed to trim the loose flap.

✓

Bucket Handle Tear

A serious type of tear where a large portion of the meniscus is displaced, often causing the knee to lock. Surgery is typically required to repair or remove the torn portion.

✓

Degenerative Tear

This type occurs in older adults due to wear and tear over time. It may be treated conservatively with rest and physical therapy, though surgery could be needed for larger tears.

✓

Complex Tear

A combination of different tear patterns, often occurring in degenerative knees. Treatment varies depending on the severity, ranging from non-surgical methods to arthroscopic surgery.

FAQs for Meniscus Tear Treatment in Ranchi

How do I know if I have a meniscus tear?
Common symptoms include knee pain, swelling, stiffness, a popping sensation, and difficulty moving the knee fully or locking of the knee.
Can a meniscus tear heal on its own?
Small tears may heal with rest and physical therapy, but larger or more severe tears often require medical treatment, including surgery.
Do I need surgery for a meniscus tear?
Surgery is usually recommended if the tear causes persistent pain, instability, or locking of the knee, or if non-surgical treatments do not provide relief.
What is arthroscopic meniscus repair?
Arthroscopic surgery is a minimally invasive procedure that uses small incisions and a camera to repair or remove the damaged part of the meniscus.
How long is the recovery from meniscus tear surgery?
Recovery typically takes 4 to 6 weeks for minor repairs and up to 3 months for more extensive surgeries. Physical therapy is often required for full recovery.
Will I be able to return to sports after meniscus tear treatment?
Yes, with proper treatment and rehabilitation, most patients can return to sports and physical activities within 3 to 6 months, depending on the severity of the tear.
Can meniscus tears lead to long-term problems?
Without proper treatment, a meniscus tear can lead to long-term knee problems, including arthritis and increased instability.
What happens if I delay treatment for a meniscus tear?
Delaying treatment can worsen the tear and cause further damage to the knee joint, leading to more pain and limited mobility.
Is meniscus surgery safe?
Yes, meniscus surgery, especially arthroscopic surgery, is considered safe and effective, with low risk of complications when performed by an experienced surgeon.
