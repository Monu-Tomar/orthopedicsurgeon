What Does a Pediatric Orthopedic Specialist Do?

A pediatric orthopedic specialist focuses on diagnosing, treating, and managing musculoskeletal problems in children, from infancy through adolescence. Unlike adults, children’s bones are still growing, making their orthopedic conditions different in both presentation and treatment.

Common pediatric orthopedic conditions include:

  • Congenital Deformities:These include conditions like clubfoot, developmental dysplasia of the hip (DDH), and limb length discrepancies that are present at birth.
  • Trauma and Fractures: Children are prone to falls and accidents, which can result in fractures or joint dislocations that require specialized treatment.
  • Growth Plate Injuries: Injuries to the growth plates, the areas of developing tissue at the ends of long bones, need immediate attention to avoid long-term complications.
  • Scoliosis and Spinal Deformities: Curvature of the spine or other spinal deformities often require early detection and intervention to prevent worsening over time.
  • Sports Injuries: Active children and adolescents are at risk for injuries such as sprains, strains, and fractures that may require surgical or non-surgical treatment.
  • Neuromuscular Disorders: Conditions like cerebral palsy and muscular dystrophy can affect a child’s muscles, bones, and joints, requiring ongoing orthopedic care.

Our children’s orthopedic surgeon in Ranchi Jharkhand is skilled in addressing these and other conditions, offering both surgical and non-surgical treatment options based on the severity of the problem and the age of the child.

Common Conditions Treated by a Children’s Orthopedic Surgeon

Children can suffer from a wide range of orthopedic issues, many of which are unique to their stage of development. A children’s orthopedic surgeon is trained to handle these conditions with a deep understanding of how a child’s musculoskeletal system grows and changes. Here are some of the most common conditions treated by a pediatric orthopedic specialist:

  1. Congenital Disorders

Congenital orthopedic conditions like clubfoot, hip dysplasia, and skeletal dysplasias (such as dwarfism) require early intervention for the best outcomes. Our specialist in children’s bone and joint conditions is experienced in correcting these issues, helping children develop properly and avoid long-term complications.

  1. Pediatric Fractures

Children’s bones are more flexible than adults’, but they are still prone to fractures, especially in high-impact sports or play. Our pediatric orthopedic specialist provides both emergency care and long-term treatment for fractures, ensuring proper healing and minimizing the risk of growth disturbances.

  1. Scoliosis

Scoliosis, or curvature of the spine, often appears during the growth spurts of adolescence. Early detection and treatment are critical to preventing severe spinal deformities. Our children’s orthopedic surgeon offers non-invasive treatments, such as bracing, as well as surgical options for more advanced cases.

  1. Limb Length Discrepancies

In cases where one limb is shorter than the other, a pediatric orthopedic specialist in Ranchi Jharkhand can offer solutions ranging from simple observation to surgical intervention, depending on the severity of the discrepancy and the child’s stage of growth.

  1. Cerebral Palsy and Neuromuscular Conditions

Children with cerebral palsy or other neuromuscular conditions often face challenges with muscle tone, joint deformities, and mobility. Our specialist in children’s bone and joint conditions works closely with a multidisciplinary team to provide tailored treatments that help improve function and quality of life.

Non-Surgical Treatments

Not all pediatric orthopedic conditions require surgery. For many conditions, non-surgical treatments can be highly effective, including:

  • Casting and Bracing:Used to correct deformities or stabilize fractures, these treatments are often the first line of defense in treating children’s orthopedic issues.
  • Physical Therapy:Rehabilitation is key to restoring strength and mobility after an injury or surgery. Our pediatric rehabilitation team works closely with each child to ensure they regain their full potential.
  • Growth Monitoring: For conditions like scoliosis or limb length discrepancies, regular monitoring of growth is critical. Our pediatric orthopedic specialist uses advanced imaging to track your child’s development and adjust treatment as needed.

Surgical Treatments

When surgery is necessary, our children’s orthopedic surgeon is highly experienced in performing procedures that are tailored to the unique needs of children. Some of the most common surgical treatments we provide include:

  • Joint Realignment: Conditions like hip dysplasia or knee deformities often require surgical correction to restore proper alignment and function.
