What is Arthroscopy Surgery?

Arthroscopy is a less invasive technique utilized for diagnosing and addressing various joint conditions. During the surgery, a small camera (arthroscope) is inserted into the joint through small incisions, enabling the surgeon to inspect and address damaged tissues effectively. without making large cuts.

This procedure is frequently carried out on joints like the knee, shoulder, hip, and ankle to treat conditions like torn ligaments, cartilage damage, and inflammation.

The procedure offers many benefits, including reduced discomfort, faster healing, and minimal scarring in contrast to traditional open surgery, making it an effective option for joint problems.

Benefits of Arthroscopy Surgery

Minimally Invasive
Arthroscopy uses small incisions, resulting in less tissue damage and minimal scarring compared to traditional surgery.
Faster Recovery
Patients generally recover faster, enabling them to resume daily routines and physical activities more quickly.
Reduced Pain and Discomfort
Smaller cuts and minimal impact on surrounding tissues lead to less post-operative pain and a more comfortable recovery.
Lower Risk of Infection
The minimally invasive nature of arthroscopy reduces the risk of infection compared to open surgery.
Outpatient Procedure
This procedure is commonly done on an outpatient basis, allowing patients to go home on the same day.
Precise Diagnosis and Treatment
The arthroscope provides a clear view of the joint, allowing the surgeon to diagnose and treat problems with high accuracy.

Types of Arthroscopy Surgeries

Knee Arthroscopy

Used to diagnose and treat knee problems like torn meniscus, ACL injuries, cartilage damage, and removal of loose fragments in the knee joint.

Shoulder Arthroscopy

Commonly performed to treat rotator cuff tears, shoulder dislocations, labral tears, and impingement syndrome, allowing for precise repairs.

Hip Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive surgery to treat hip impingement, labral tears, and cartilage damage, improving mobility and reducing pain.

Ankle Arthroscopy

Used to diagnose and treat ligament injuries, cartilage damage, and impingement in the ankle joint, enhancing mobility and joint function.

Wrist Arthroscopy

Helps in diagnosing and treating wrist ligament injuries, fractures, or inflammation, restoring function and reducing wrist pain.

Elbow Arthroscopy

Frequently utilized for addressing conditions such as tennis elbow, loose bodies in the joint, and arthritis, providing long-term relief and improved joint movement.

Why is Arthroscopy Surgery Performed?

Removing Damaged Tissue or Loose Bodies

The procedure can remove inflamed or damaged tissue, bone fragments, or loose cartilage that cause joint discomfort.

Repairing Ligament and Cartilage Tears

It is often used to treat torn ligaments, such as ACL injuries, and repair damaged cartilage in the knee, shoulder, hip, or ankle.

Treating Sports Injuries

Arthroscopy is commonly used to treat sports-related injuries like rotator cuff tears, meniscus tears, and joint impingements.

Diagnosing Joint Issues

Arthroscopy helps identify the cause of pain, stiffness, and swelling by providing a clear view inside the joint.

Addressing Joint Inflammation

It can help treat conditions like synovitis (inflammation of the joint lining) by removing inflamed tissue.

FAQs About Arthroscopy Surgery in Ranchi

Which joints can be treated with arthroscopy?
Arthroscopy can be performed on joints such as the knee, shoulder, hip, ankle, wrist, and elbow.
Is arthroscopy surgery painful?
Arthroscopy is less painful than traditional surgery due to smaller incisions. Post-surgery discomfort is usually minimal and manageable with medication.
What is the recovery time for arthroscopy surgery?
Most patients can return to light activities within a few days to a few weeks, with full recovery typically taking 4 to 6 weeks.
Do I need physical therapy after arthroscopy?
Yes, physical therapy is often recommended after arthroscopy to help restore strength, mobility, and joint function.
Is arthroscopy an outpatient procedure?
Yes, the majority of arthroscopy procedures are done on an outpatient basis, allowing patients to return home the same day.
