AC Joint Repair and Reconstruction in Ranchi: Expert Orthopedic Care by Dr. Ankur Saurav

A common approach for treating shoulder injuries, AC Joint Repair Treatment targets damage to the acromioclavicular (AC) joint. The AC joint, which connects the collarbone (clavicle) to the acromion (part of the scapula), is prone to injury, especially among athletes and individuals involved in heavy lifting or high-impact activities.

When conservative treatments fail, AC joint repair is often the best solution to restore function and alleviate pain. Here, we will explore the benefits of shoulder AC joint injury repair, the effectiveness of arthroscopic AC joint repair, and important considerations such as AC joint injury symptoms and the AC joint repair success rate.

What is AC Joint Repair?

AC joint repair is a surgical procedure designed to treat injuries affecting the acromioclavicular (AC) joint. These injuries are typically the result of trauma, such as a fall onto the shoulder, repetitive overhead motions, or accidents. The repair may involve the reconstruction of ligaments, removal of damaged tissue, or the reattachment of torn ligaments to restore stability to the joint.

If non-surgical treatments such as physical therapy, medications, and rest are ineffective, surgery is recommended. This surgical approach allows the patient to regain full shoulder function and reduces the pain caused by the damaged AC joint.

AC Joint Repair Causes and Diagnosis

A shoulder AC joint repair Treatment is necessary when the ligaments in the AC joint are damaged, often resulting in a condition known as AC joint separation. This injury can vary in severity, from mild sprains to complete tears.
Common causes of AC joint injuries include:

  • Trauma or direct impact: A fall on the shoulder or a strong blow can lead to injury of the AC joint.
  • Repetitive use: Athletes who engage in overhead motions, such as weightlifting or baseball, are at a higher risk.
  • Degeneration: Over time, the wear and tear on the AC joint can cause injury, particularly in older individuals.

Symptoms of AC joint injury include pain at the top of the shoulder, swelling, a visible bump or deformity in severe cases, and limited range of motion. Diagnosing an AC joint injury typically involves a physical examination and imaging tests like X-rays or MRIs to determine the extent of the damage.

Arthroscopic Procedure: A Minimally Invasive Approach

This minimally invasive surgery involves making small incisions and using an arthroscope, a tiny camera, to assist in guiding the procedure. Through these tiny incisions, the surgeon can repair or remove damaged tissue in the joint without the need for large cuts. Arthroscopic AC joint repair provides the following benefits:

  1. Reduced scarring: Smaller incisions lead to less noticeable scarring compared to traditional open surgery.
  2. Reduced pain and recovery time: Since the surgery is less invasive, patients typically experience less postoperative pain and a quicker recovery.
  3. Quicker recovery for daily activities: Arthroscopic surgery promotes faster healing, enabling patients to return to work and sports activities more quickly.

With its precise technique, arthroscopic surgery boosts the success rate and reduces potential complications.

AC Joint Injury Symptoms: How to Recognize the Damage

Recognizing AC joint injury symptoms early can help in determining the need for repair:

  1. Shoulder pain: The most common symptom, usually concentrated at the top of the shoulder.
  2. MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging): You may notice swelling or bruising around the AC joint.
  3. Restricted range of motion: Shoulder movement may be limited, and lifting the arm could lead to discomfort or pain.
  4. Visible deformity: In severe cases like AC joint separation, a prominent bump may develop where the collarbone meets the shoulder blade.

If these symptoms persist or worsen despite conservative treatments, it may be time to consult with an orthopedic surgeon about AC joint repair options.

What to Expect During and After AC Joint Repair Surgery

During the AC joint repair procedure, the surgeon will either perform an arthroscopic or open repair, depending on the severity of the injury. The procedure generally takes 45 minutes to an hour, and most patients can return home the next day of surgery.

Post-surgery, recovery will include:

  1. Immobilization: The shoulder may be placed in a sling to allow the joint to heal and to limit movement.
  2. Physical Therapy: After the healing process begins, physical therapy will assist in rebuilding strength and flexibility in the shoulder.
  3. Pain management: Medications will be prescribed to control pain and reduce swelling.

It is important for patients to follow their surgeon’s recommendations during the recovery process to ensure a smooth and successful recovery.

Success Rate and Outcomes

The AC joint repair success rate is generally high, especially when the injury is treated promptly and by an experienced orthopedic surgeon. Factors that influence the success rate include the severity of the injury, the surgical technique used, and adherence to post-surgery rehabilitation.

For those undergoing arthroscopic AC joint repair, success rates are particularly favorable. Most patients experience:

  1. Pain relief: After surgery, pain associated with AC joint injury typically diminishes significantly.
  2. Restored mobility:With physical therapy, patients regain their full range of motion and strength.
  3. Return to activities: Many patients can return to normal activities, including sports, within a few months after surgery.

Restoring Shoulder Function with AC Joint Repair

AC joint injuries can be debilitating, but with the right treatment, full recovery is possible. Whether through arthroscopic AC joint repair or traditional surgery, AC joint repair provides a pathway to relieve pain and restore function. By understanding the causes, symptoms, and recovery process, patients can make informed decisions about their treatment. Choosing a highly skilled surgeon and following post-surgical rehabilitation guidelines will significantly enhance the chances of a successful outcome.
