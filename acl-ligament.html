What is ACL Surgery?

ACL surgery involves reconstructing or repairing a damaged ACL in the knee. The ACL is a crucial ligament that provides stability to the knee joint, and when it’s torn, this can result in knee instability, causing pain and difficulty with movement.

ACL injuries can occur during sports or activities involving sudden changes in direction, jumping, or rapid stops. During ACL surgery, the damaged ligament is replaced with a graft, either from the patient’s own tissue or from a donor. This surgery helps restore knee stability, allowing patients to return to daily activities and sports.

Symptoms of ACL Tear

A Popping Sensation

Many people hear or feel a “pop” in the knee at the time of injury.

Immediate Pain

Severe pain usually follows the injury, making it difficult to continue physical activities.

Swelling

Swelling typically develops a few hours after the injury as a result of bleeding inside the knee joint.

Knee Instability

The knee may feel unstable or like it’s “giving way” when walking or standing.

When is ACL Surgery Needed?

ACL surgery is recommended in the following situations:

Complete ACL Tear

If the ACL is completely torn and cannot heal on its own, surgery is often required to restore knee stability.

Knee Instability

If the knee frequently "gives way" during physical activities or normal movement, surgery may be needed to regain stability and function.

Active Lifestyle

Those who participate in athletic activities or sports-related movements physically demanding activities often require surgery to safely return to their active lifestyle.

Failure of Non-Surgical Treatments

When non-invasive treatments like bracing and physical therapy fail to restore knee function and stability, surgery becomes the best option.

Associated Knee Injuries

Surgery may be necessary if other knee structures, such as the meniscus or cartilage, are also damaged alongside the ACL tear.

Types of Hip Replacement Surgeries

✓

Autograft ACL Reconstruction

This technique uses tissue from the patient’s own body, typically from the hamstring tendon or patellar tendon, to replace the damaged ACL. It is often preferred for active individuals and athletes due to its durability.

✓

Allograft ACL Reconstruction

In this method, donor tissue (usually from a cadaver) is used to replace the torn ACL. This option may be recommended for older patients or those looking for a shorter recovery time, as it avoids the need to harvest tissue from the patient’s own body.

✓

Minimally Invasive Arthroscopic ACL Surgery

Arthroscopic ACL surgery involves smaller incisions and the use of a camera (arthroscope) to guide the procedure. It reduces post-surgical pain, scarring, and recovery time while providing high precision for repairing the ligament.

FAQs About Most advanced and less invasive ACL tear surgery in Ranchi

Do I need ACL surgery if I have a torn ACL?
Surgery is usually recommended for complete ACL tears, especially if you experience knee instability or wish to return to sports or active lifestyles.
How long does ACL surgery take?
ACL surgery typically takes around 1 to 2 hours, depending on the complexity of the procedure.
Is ACL surgery painful?
Post-surgery discomfort is common, but pain is usually managed with medications and decreases as the knee heals.
What is the difference between autograft and allograft ACL reconstruction?
An autograft uses the patient’s own tissue (usually from the hamstring or patellar tendon), while an allograft uses donor tissue from a cadaver.
When can I return to sports after ACL surgery?
Patients typically return to sports within 9 to 12 months, depending on the success of surgery and rehabilitation.
Are there risks involved in ACL surgery?
As with any surgery, there are risks such as infection, stiffness, or blood clots, but these are rare and manageable with proper care.
Will I need physical therapy after ACL surgery?
Yes, physical therapy is crucial for restoring strength, stability, and range of motion in the knee after ACL surgery.
